Research Focus

Statistical learning for changing distributions.

My current research focuses on transfer learning, particularly addressing challenges related to distributional shift, out-of-distribution detection, and label noise. I approach these problems through the lens of mixture models and density ratios.

01

Transfer Learning

Learning from related source data while accounting for mismatch between source and target populations.

02

Mixture Models

Modeling heterogeneous populations and latent substructure with interpretable statistical tools.

03

Density Ratios

Using density ratio ideas to represent shifts, estimate population differences, and build robust procedures.

04

Distributional Shift

Studying covariate, label, group-label, open-set, and continuous target shift settings.

05

Out-of-Distribution Detection

Developing methods for identifying observations that deviate from the data-generating setting.

06

Label Noise

Building inference and classification methods under noisy labels, weak supervision, and positive-unlabeled data.
